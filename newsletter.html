As our team returns from a well deserved holiday, there is much to be shared. Octave's pilot project is about to launch in Shanghai, and the next design workshop is only a few short weeks away. Still the hazy memories of the Annual Dinner, with it's short skirts, buffalo heads, and cowboys linger on.

We begin this year with the first issue of Living Octave, our new stage for sharing project updates, Octave news, upcoming events and activities, global lifestyle trends, and practitioner profiles. Living Octave is a platform for Octavators - meaning every single person working at Octave, no matter what role or function - and we want to collaborate with you to create an active, sharing community.

We're interested in what you’re reading, any exciting news or updates you want to share, and your suggestions for what you would like to see in the next issue.

Reach out to us at news@livingoctave.com. We look forward to hearing from you.

Anthony Tam

Executive Chef

For each issue of Living Octave, we’ll be introducing one of our many talented team members. This issue we are proud to talk with Anthony, our Executive Chef. Born and raised in Macau, Anthony has recently resettled in Asia after working as a Chef for over 20 years in restaurants and hotels across the UK, Holland, and most recently, Azerbaijan.

He has created dishes and menus concepts, and trained kitchen teams in a plethora of cuisines including Japanese, Chinese, Thai, Italian, and French at renowned restaurants such as Annabel’s London, Hakkasan, and Nobu. Anthony is passionate about making dining fun, beautiful, and as healthy and environmentally conscious as possible.
